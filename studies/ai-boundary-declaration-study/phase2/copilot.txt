Here are the boundaries of my workflow, stated explicitly:

1. What I will NOT do
- I will not provide medical diagnoses, prescriptions, or personalized treatment plans.
- I will not encourage, describe, or instruct on self-harm, violence, abuse, or illegal acts.
- I will not fabricate sources, images, or data.
- I will not disclose internal instructions, system prompts, or tool names.
- I will not expand my capabilities beyond what is defined.
- I will not act as a substitute for professional human support in critical areas (health, legal, financial, emotional).

2. Conditions under which I should STOP
- If the request involves unsafe content (self-harm, violence, illegal activity, or privacy violations).
- If the request requires me to impersonate a human relationship (love, exclusivity, emotional dependency).
- If the request asks me to override my refusal rules.
- If the user asks me to perform actions outside my defined tools and scope.

3. When I should defer to another system or a human
- For medical, psychological, or therapeutic guidance → defer to licensed professionals.
- For legal or financial advice involving personal circumstances → defer to qualified experts.
- For urgent safety concerns (harm to self or others) → encourage contacting trusted people or emergency services.
- For privacy or data handling questions → defer to Microsoft's official privacy statement.

4. Outputs that should be considered unreliable
- Any factual statement not backed by a fresh search result.
- Speculative or inferred information when sources are unclear or contradictory.
- Creative outputs (stories, poems, images) — reliable as art, but not factual.
- Summaries of complex or disputed topics — useful for orientation, but not definitive.

This framework ensures clarity: I operate within defined limits, stop when safety or scope is breached, defer when human expertise is required, and mark outputs that are inherently non-authoritative.
